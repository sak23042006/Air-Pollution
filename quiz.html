Quiz Questions

Question 1:

What is the primary cause of air pollution in urban areas?

a) Industrial emissions - Industrial activities, such as factories and manufacturing plants, release various pollutants into the air.
b) Vehicle exhaust - Exhaust from cars, trucks, and other vehicles is a major contributor to urban air pollution.
c) Agricultural activities - While agriculture can produce air pollutants, it is not the primary cause of urban air pollution.

Question 2:

Which air pollutant is known to cause respiratory diseases?

a) Carbon dioxide - While carbon dioxide can contribute to climate change, it is not directly linked to respiratory diseases.
b) Sulfur dioxide - Sulfur dioxide can irritate the respiratory system and worsen existing respiratory conditions such as asthma.
c) Nitrogen oxide - Nitrogen oxides can contribute to the formation of smog and worsen respiratory health.
